About

From mountain environments to river systems

My work connects remote sensing, geospatial technologies, and data-driven environmental analysis across climate, hazards, land systems, and inland waters.

I am currently a doctoral researcher affiliated with Université de Picardie Jules Verne and UniLaSalle Beauvais, working on the prediction of water quality to allow swimming in the Seine in real time through the qualification of multivariate online data, development of indicators, and modelling based on satellite imagery.

My broader research interests include the integration of remote sensing and geospatial technologies for climate-change-related environmental monitoring, water resources, hazard vulnerability, and spatial modelling. I also work with machine learning approaches to analyse Earth observation data and identify patterns relevant to environmental decision-making.

Across my academic and professional journey, I have worked in Pakistan, Hong Kong, and France, with experience spanning GIS operations, land and water monitoring, InSAR-based deformation studies, land cover modelling, and systematic review work in remote sensing and environmental applications.

Research

Core research areas

My current work sits at the intersection of Earth observation, geospatial analysis, and predictive environmental modelling.

🌊

River Water Quality Monitoring

Satellite and in situ data integration for water-quality assessment, indicator development, and real-time modelling applications.

🛰️

Remote Sensing of Inland Waters

Use of Sentinel imagery, reflectance products, and processing workflows for monitoring optically complex river systems.

🧠

Machine Learning for Earth Observation

Predictive modelling and pattern analysis from large-scale Earth observation datasets for environmental applications.

🗺️

GIS & Spatial Analysis

Spatial analysis, geostatistical modelling, mapping, and decision-support workflows using desktop and cloud-based GIS tools.

📡

InSAR & Ground Deformation

Land subsidence and terrain deformation studies using synthetic aperture radar interferometry and geospatial interpretation.

🌍

Climate, Hazards & Environmental Change

Applications of remote sensing to hazard vulnerability, climate impacts, and landscape-level environmental monitoring.
